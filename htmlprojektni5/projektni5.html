Tim Berners-Lee is a British computer scientist who invented the World Wide Web, revolutionizing how we access and share information online.

Born on June 8, 1955, Berners-Lee grew up in London and later studied physics at Oxford University. He became a software engineer and worked at CERN, where the idea of the World Wide Web first emerged.

In 1989, Berners-Lee published the concept of a global information-sharing system, integrating the Internet with hypertext. His proposal led to the creation of the first web browser, and the first website, info.cern.ch, was launched in 1991.

In 1994, Berners-Lee founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) at MIT to oversee the development of web standards, ensuring the web's long-term growth and accessibility.
